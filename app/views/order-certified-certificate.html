Order a certified certificate

Use this service to order a paper or digital signed certificate for a company.

You'll need:

  • to sign in to or create a Companies House account
  • a debit or credit card

To order for a different company, search for the company on the register.

This certificate is different to the incorporation documents that were issued when the company was formed. Copies of these can be ordered through the Order a certified document service.

Choosing paper or digital

A certificate includes certified details about a company. The information you can include will depend on whether you order a paper or digital certificate.

A paper certificate will include basic information, including the company name, company number and incorporation date. You can choose to add more information about the company, for example a statement of good standing.

For now, digital certificates will include set company information and cannot be customised. They will not include a statement of good standing. If you need this, you should order a paper certificate instead.

Read the guidance to find out what information can be included on paper and digital certificates.

Check if a digital certificate will be accepted

You should check that a digital certificate with an electronic Companies House signature will be accepted by:

  • the person or organisation asking for the certificate (for example, the bank or embassy)
  • your notary, if you are getting the certificate notarised
How are paper and digital certificates certified?

Paper copies will be signed with a blue, wet ink signature by one of our officials.

Digital copies will be a PDF document, embedded with an approved Companies House electronic signature. You will need to be open the PDF in Adobe reader to view the signature.

An electronic signature will not look like a wet ink signature. It will show the Companies House seal and the date the document was embedded with the electronic signature.
