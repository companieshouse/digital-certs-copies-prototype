For companies on the Companies House register, you can order a:

  • paper or digital certified copy of a document in the company's filing history
  • certificate of incorporation for a company, including all company name changes

How to order

You’ll need to use the Find and update company information service to search for the company you want to order certified documents or certificates for, then:

  • go to the company overview page
  • select the ‘More’ tab

You can also order by calling the Companies House contact centre on 0303 1234 500.

Choosing paper or digital documents

You can order paper copies of certified documents or certificates. Depending on your order, we may be able to offer digital certified certificates or documents.

You should contact the person asking for the certificate or document to check what they need before ordering.

How are paper and digital documents certified?
Paper copies will be signed in person by one of our officials. Digital copies will be a PDF document, signed with an approved Companies House electronic signature.

Costs and delivery

Digital service

It costs £XX for a digital certified document. Incorporation orders cost £XX.

Your order will be available to download within 1 hour.

Standard paper service

It costs £15 for a certified document with standard dispatch. Incorporation orders cost £30.

We will aim to send out the certificate within 10 working days.

We send UK orders by Royal Mail 2nd Class post and international orders by Royal Mail International post.

Express paper service

It costs £50 for a certified document with express dispatch. The incorporation packages cost £100 with express dispatch.

Orders received before 11am will be sent out the same day. Orders received after 11am will be sent out the next working day.

We send UK orders by Royal Mail 1st Class post and international orders by Royal Mail International post.

Ordering incorporation certificates

You can request to include additional certified facts on an incorporation certificate. These are:

  • directors’ names, and details such as date of birth or nationality
  • secretaries’ names
  • registered office address
  • the company’s objects
  • good standing statement

The good standing wording says that a company has been in continuous, unbroken existence since its incorporation and that no action is currently being taken to strike the company off the register.

We’ll only issue a good standing statement if the company is up to date with its filings. It must be a:

  • private limited company with at least one director who’s a natural person
  • public limited company with a secretary and at least 2 directors (one must be a natural person
