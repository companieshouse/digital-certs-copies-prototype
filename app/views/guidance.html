For companies on the Companies House register, you can order a:

  • certified copy of a document in the company's filing history
  • package of certified incorporation documents
  • certified certificate which includes company details and has the option to add more information, such as a statement of good standing

How to order

You’ll need to use the Find and update company information service to search for the company you want to order certified documents or certificates for, then:

  • go to the company overview page
  • select the ‘More’ tab

You can also order by calling the Companies House contact centre on 0303 1234 500.

Ordering a certified document

A certified document is a certified copy of a document from the company filing history.

You can order a paper certified document for any company on the register. You can choose to order a digital certified document for Limited companies that:

  • were incorporated under the Companies Act 2006
  • have not had a name change
  • have not re-registered to a different company type

Incorporation packages

An incorporation package includes all the documents from when a company was formed, including a copy of the certificate of incorporation.

You need to use the Order a certified document service to order an incorporation package.

Ordering a certified certificate

A certified certificate can include key details about a company. The information you can include will depend on whether you order a paper or digital certificate.

Paper certified certificates

A paper certificate includes a company’s name and any name changes, company number and the incorporation date. You can request to add company information, including:

  • directors’ names, and details such as date of birth or nationality
  • secretaries’ names
  • current and previous registered office address
  • the company’s objects
  • good standing statement
What is a statement of good standing?

The good standing wording says that a company has been in continuous, unbroken existence since its incorporation and that no action is currently being taken to strike the company off the register.

We’ll only issue a good standing statement if the company is up to date with its filings. It must be a:

  • private limited company with at least one director who’s a natural person
  • public limited company with a secretary and at least 2 directors (one must be a natural person)

You cannot order certificates with information on stakeholders shareholdings or statement of capital.

Digital certified certificates

For now, digital certificates will include set company information and cannot be customised. They will not include a statement of good standing. If you need this, you should order a paper certificate instead.

A digital certificate will include the:

  • company name and number
  • incorporation date
  • current registered office address
  • current directors
  • current secretaries
  • nature of business

Choosing paper or digital

Paper certificates and documents will be signed with a blue, wet ink signature by one of our officials.

Digital certificates and documents will be a PDF document, embedded with an approved Companies House electronic signature. They are only valid in digital form and can be shared by email.

You should contact the person asking for the certificate or document to check what they need before ordering.

Legalise a document or certificate

You can get a document legalised with the Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Office (FCDO). In countries who have signed up to the Hague Convention of 1961, this gives the document the same authenticity it has in the UK.

To get a Companies House document legalised by the FCDO, it must have the original signature of a British public official, or an embedded electronic Companies House Signature. To get a document signed in person by one of our officials, you’ll need to request a certified certificate or certified document.
